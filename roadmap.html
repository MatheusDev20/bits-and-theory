Networking, databases, and operating systems are separate study tracks — but system design is where they converge. Ride each line in order; the last one only makes sense once the first three are behind you.

Phase 2 · Databases line ⏱ 5–6 weeks

The highest-leverage track for a full stack dev

Best practical exercise: load a few million rows into Postgres, write slow queries on purpose, fix them while reading EXPLAIN ANALYZE.

DoOne real Postgres database, millions of rows, slow queries fixed with indexes. Teaches more than any course.

Phase 4 · System design — the interchange ⏱ 2–3 months, ongoing

Where the three lines converge

With phases 1–3 done, these stop being buzzwords. Practice on systems you already use: a URL shortener, a rate limiter, a notification system, a news feed — with capacity estimates, schema, API, failure modes.

Read slowlyDesigning Data-Intensive Applications — Martin Kleppmann. This entire map in book form; one chapter at a time, after phases 1–2.

How to make it stick: tie every topic back to systems you've already built. When you learn isolation levels, check what your production database uses and whether it explains a bug you've seen. Your six years of scar tissue is the advantage you have over someone learning this fresh.
